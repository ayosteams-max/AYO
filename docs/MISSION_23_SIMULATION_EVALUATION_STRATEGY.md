# Mission 23 — Simulation and Evaluation Strategy

Use synthetic and separately approved de-identified event snapshots. Replay identical
inputs through baseline and candidate policies with seeded tie-breaks. Model weak networks,
GPS error, closures, worker death, demand spikes, airport queues, current-trip delay and
scheduled commitments. Shadow mode writes isolated decisions only.

Measure median/p90/p95/p99 pickup, assignment success, scheduled on-time pickup,
redispatch/cancellation, driver idle and opportunity, earnings-opportunity proxy, airport
queue, acceptance latency, weak-network recovery, pre-dispatch benefit, calibration,
support disputes and rider/driver satisfaction.

Gates require no safety/authority/commitment invariant regression, statistically supported
pickup/reliability benefit, bounded fairness disparity, calibrated predictions, privacy
approval, operational capacity and tested rollback. Numeric thresholds require Addis
baseline and leadership approval. Any critical violation immediately reverts to baseline.
