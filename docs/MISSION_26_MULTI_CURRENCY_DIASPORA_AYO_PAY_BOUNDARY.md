# Mission 26 — Multi-Currency, Diaspora and Future AYO Pay Boundary

ETB is the primary launch currency. Every amount carries ISO currency and minor-unit
definition. Accounts are single-currency. Journal balancing occurs per currency; an FX
transaction uses explicit source, destination, clearing, rate/quote version, spread/fee,
rounding and gain/loss treatment approved by Finance and law.

Diaspora payments separate payer, rider/beneficiary, booking owner, payment instrument,
refund recipient and consent. Cross-border collection, foreign exchange, sanctions/AML,
tax, data transfer, chargeback and consumer disclosures require provider and qualified
Ethiopian/international legal approval. No currency conversion is authorized here.

Driver/rider/business “wallet” is an internal accounting projection only. Transferable,
redeemable, prefunded or broadly spendable value may create regulated stored-value or
payment-service obligations. Future AYO Pay therefore requires a separate mission,
licensing strategy, safeguarding, governance, capital/liquidity, AML/CFT and operational
architecture; it cannot inherit Mission 26 approval.
