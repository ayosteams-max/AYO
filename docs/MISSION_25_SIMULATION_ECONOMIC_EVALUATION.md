# Mission 25 — Simulation and Economic Evaluation Strategy

Use synthetic or governance-approved de-identified trips; never production identity or
raw location in external AI tools. Scenarios span Immediate/Scheduled, airport products,
cash/digital boundaries, weak networks, route failure, cancellations, waiting evidence,
fuel/maintenance/tax/payment-cost sensitivity and promotion attacks.

Measure affordability by zone/service, driver earnings per active hour and dispersion,
idle time, contribution margin, assignment/cancellation/no-show disputes, airport
reliability, incentive/promotion cost and fraud, supply, support contacts, estimate error
(median/tail and direction), satisfaction and fairness cohorts. Report bonuses separately
so they cannot conceal negative base economics.

Compare the static baseline against any candidate policy with pre-registered thresholds,
confidence intervals and worst-case sensitivity. Shadow mode creates no user-facing
price. Launch needs leadership-approved thresholds, driver/rider research and Finance/
Legal sign-off. Zero-tolerance blockers are hidden negative driver economics, misleading
price, discriminatory inputs/outcomes, uncapped surge, unsafe incentive effects,
unauthorized financial authority and retroactive unexplained change. Roll back by
stopping new policy selection and returning to the last approved version; accepted and
completed trips retain their snapshots.
