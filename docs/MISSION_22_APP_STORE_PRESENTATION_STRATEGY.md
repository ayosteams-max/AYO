# Mission 22 — App Store Presentation Strategy

Date: 2026-07-16
Status: **Strategy proposal only. No listing, asset or submission authorized.**

## Positioning

Position AYO through its own verifiable strengths, not competitor names or unverifiable
“best/#1” claims: Ethiopia-first pickup clarity; bilingual landmark/entrance guidance;
calm weak-network recovery; transparent server-confirmed ride status; rider and driver
fairness; accessible airport Standard/Premium distinction; and visible evidence-based
waiting only after it is certified and shipped.

Do not market broad super-app services, AI automation, Mission 20, external maps,
payments, voice/video support or safety outcomes until the reviewed build actually ships
them. Use fictional names, plates, places/account details and approved brand assets.

## Release-evidence gate

Each claim and screenshot maps to a release-manifest capability, test evidence, locale,
privacy disclosure and owner approval. Screenshots are captured from the submitted build
or a policy-compliant, clearly accurate capture workflow. Remove a panel when its feature
flag is off. Never present a planned screen as live.

## Proposed screenshot story after corresponding features ship

1. “Book clearly in Amharic or English” — destination and pickup confirmation.
2. “Meet at the right entrance” — named landmark and exact pickup point.
3. “Know what is confirmed” — assigned driver and server-status clarity.
4. “Recover when the network drops” — timestamped reconnect state.
5. “Pickup guidance for complex places” — airport/venue journey.
6. “Safety and Help stay visible” — non-alarmist safety access.
7. Driver listing: fair offer clarity, exact stop and recoverable task state.

Rider and Driver apps should use separate listings if their audiences, permissions and
workflows remain distinct. Leadership must approve final packaging.

## Store constraints reviewed

Apple requires accurate, current metadata/screenshots reflecting the core experience,
fictional rather than real account data, and no unverifiable claims or references to
other apps in subtitles. Apple permits up to ten screenshots; the first one to three are
especially important in search when no preview is present. Google Play prohibits
misleading or comparative metadata, supports up to eight phone screenshots, requires
accurate preview assets and recommends useful alt text. Requirements change and must be
rechecked immediately before submission.

## Measurement

Use store experiments only after adequate traffic and privacy review. Measure qualified
install conversion, onboarding completion, first successful booking, uninstall/crash and
support-contact rates by locale/device—not installs alone. Never manipulate reviews or
filter dissatisfied users. Store optimization cannot override truthful representation.
