# Mission 22 — Accessibility and Content Standard

Status: **Documentation proposal; native-user validation required.**

- Lead with what is happening, then what the person should do.
- Use simple verbs and short sentences; internal states/reason codes never appear.
- Never use urgency to obscure price, waiting status, consent or cancellation review.
- Every map marker, icon, color and animation has a text/accessibility equivalent.
- Countdown announces meaningful intervals without continuous screen-reader interruption.
- Driver moving-mode screens expose only glanceable status and safe large actions.
- Amharic and English are authored/verified experiences, not automatic literal copies.
- Numerals, time, plate, terminal and landmark forms are tested with local users.
- Accessibility preferences persist without implying diagnosis or protected traits.
- Unsafe/unreachable pickup and emergency paths remain reachable without precision taps.
- Recovery copy states last confirmed time and whether an action is pending or confirmed.
- Destructive actions identify outcome uncertainty and Support/appeal path.

Prototype acceptance requires keyboard/switch navigation where applicable, screen-reader
order, text scaling, contrast, reduced motion, landscape/small-screen resilience,
sunlight evaluation and one-handed task completion while parked.
