# Mission 25 — Waiting, Cancellation and No-Show Policy Architecture

## Consequence gate

A possible rider consequence requires activated/certified Mission 20 evidence of
verified arrival, pickup continuity, expired configured free wait, reasonable
notification, valid guidance, sufficient quality and no unresolved platform failure or
unfair external disruption. Otherwise Pricing suppresses or routes review. A timer alone
is never sufficient.

Separate policy outcomes cover free wait, possible paid wait, rider no-show, driver
no-show, paused/cancelled/invalidated wait, suppression and manual review. No duration or
amount is approved. Paid wait, cancellation and no-show components cannot stack unless
an expressly approved policy proves no double counting.

Rider cancellation contexts distinguish pre-assignment, grace period, material driver
travel, verified arrival, Scheduled, Airport Standard/Premium, driver lateness/movement,
bad guidance, platform failure, emergency/external disruption and insufficient evidence.
Driver cancellation evidence distinguishes pre-travel, post-acceptance, breakdown,
safety, misconduct, inaccessible pickup, map/platform failure, road restriction,
commitment and airport access failure. Outputs are eligibility/review reason codes only;
no hidden punishment or compensation payment is authorized.

Governing principle: AYO prevents cancellations; it does not optimize revenue from them.
