# Mission 25 — Airport Standard and Premium Pricing Architecture

Airport Standard and Airport Premium use separate service and policy identities for
city-to-airport, airport-to-city, scheduled and third-party traveller bookings. Inputs
may reference terminal/zone, approved access/parking costs, waiting-policy identity,
flight-context freshness and formal assignment state.

Premium must correspond to an approved higher service—such as vehicle class, Trusted
Driver eligibility or service reliability—and never nationality, language or diaspora
status. Comparison before confirmation shows included service, estimate, material
conditions and expiry. A driver ETA becomes final only after formal assignment; no
specific driver is promised earlier.

Flight delay, congestion and closure are evidence inputs to approved suppression or
review policy, not autonomous repricing authority. Airport fees, guarantees and numeric
premiums require airport/transport/legal evidence and leadership approval.
