# Mission 24 — Trust Decision Explainability and Appeal

Every decision includes decision/case/identity ID, purpose, decision type, policy/version,
evidence references and freshness, assurance result, reason/missing-evidence codes,
expiry/reverification, prohibited actions, escalation/appeal status, actor/authority and
timestamp. Corrections append a superseding decision.

Rider/Driver explanations use simple English/Amharic: what was checked, result, what is
missing/expired, safe next action, review timing and appeal path. They do not reveal fraud
rules or sensitive third-party data. Support sees a purpose-scoped timeline; specialists
see only their queue fields; Operations sees aggregates.

Appeal creates an idempotent linked case, freezes no unrelated service automatically,
accepts governed evidence metadata, assigns an independent authorized reviewer and records
uphold/overturn/partial/new-evidence outcome. AI may summarize but cannot decide.
